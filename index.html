Overview

This e-portfolio presents a structured record of my work across the machine learning module. It brings together weekly artefacts, team project work, model outputs and a final reflection in order to show how my understanding developed over the duration of the module. The site is organised so that the reader can move from early foundations in machine learning and exploratory data analysis through to project implementation, evaluation and reflection.

The portfolio contains evidence from Unit 1 to Unit 12, with particular emphasis on the two assessed project stages. Unit 6 documents the initial proposal and planned analytical approach. Unit 11 documents the final implementation and the results achieved. The evaluation page compares these two stages to show how the project changed in response to data, modelling choices and team discussion.

How the portfolio is organised

  • Unit pages summarise the focus of each week and include relevant artefacts or outputs.
  • Project pages document the Unit 6 proposal, the Unit 11 final project and the CNN object recognition work.
  • Project evaluation compares the original plan with the final implementation.
  • Reflection considers technical development, teamwork, professional growth and future application.
  • References lists the main sources used across the portfolio in Harvard style.

What the portfolio shows

The evidence in this portfolio shows progression across several areas: understanding of different machine learning techniques, ability to apply methods to practical datasets, awareness of ethical and professional issues, and participation in team-based work. The combination of artefacts and reflective writing is intended to show not only what was produced, but also how my approach changed as the module progressed.
