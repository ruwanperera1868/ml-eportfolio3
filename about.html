About this portfolio

This portfolio was developed as the end-of-module e-portfolio submission for the machine learning module. Its purpose is to bring together evidence of technical learning, project work, collaboration and critical reflection in a single, accessible site.

The structure reflects the rhythm of the module. Early units focus on core concepts such as machine learning foundations, exploratory data analysis, correlation and regression. Middle units move into clustering and neural networks. Later units consider convolutional neural networks, evaluation and the broader implications of Industry 4.0. The project pages and reflection are intended to connect these weekly activities into a coherent learning journey rather than a collection of isolated tasks.

Portfolio contents

  • Weekly evidence from Unit 1 to Unit 12
  • Unit 6 proposal and Unit 11 final project documentation
  • CNN object recognition work
  • Comparative evaluation of proposal versus final delivery
  • Final reflection
  • Harvard-style references

Approach taken

My aim was to present the material clearly and honestly. Where the module required practical outputs, I have included charts, diagrams and model results. Where the module required reflection, I have tried to focus on what changed in my understanding rather than simply summarising what I did. The portfolio therefore combines descriptive evidence with analytical commentary.
